Notebook 16 – Scenario Analysis and Conclusions

Objective

The objective of this notebook is to compare the performance of flexible EV charging against ASAP charging for representative daily scenarios.

Three representative days were evaluated:

- Low-demand day (Day 106)
- Medium-demand day (Day 20)
- High-demand day (Day 95)

Results

Low-demand scenario:
EV energy = 37 kWh
Cost saving = 2.27 EUR/day
Grid import reduction = 0 kWh/day
Peak impact = 0 kW

The PV+BESS system is sufficiently large to absorb the EV charging demand. Flexible charging mainly improves the utilization of locally generated energy and slightly improves economic performance.

Medium-demand scenario:
EV energy = 303 kWh
Cost saving = 1.29 EUR/day
Grid import reduction = 3.46 kWh/day
Peak impact = +5.94 kW

Flexible charging reduces operating cost and grid energy consumption. However, because the objective is 
cost-oriented, charging is shifted toward cheaper periods, which increases peak demand.

High-demand scenario:
EV energy = 509 kWh
Cost saving = 4.48 EUR/day
Grid import reduction = 0 kWh/day
Peak impact = 0 kW

The high PV production allows most EV charging demand to be supplied locally. Flexible charging further 
improves the economic performance by better aligning charging with PV generation.

Conclusions

Flexible EV charging consistently outperforms ASAP charging in terms of operating cost.

The largest benefit is achieved when high PV production is available.

The medium-demand scenario is the most critical operating condition because PV generation is limited and 
grid dependency is highest.

Cost-oriented charging does not necessarily reduce peak demand and may increase peak grid import.

The optimally sized PV+BESS system absorbs a large fraction of EV charging demand, reducing the economic 
impact of vehicle charging on the parking lot energy system.

Overall, the results demonstrate the operational and economic benefits of predictive smart charging 
compared with non-controllable ASAP charging.
