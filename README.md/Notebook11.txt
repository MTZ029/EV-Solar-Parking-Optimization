Integrated PV + BESS + Flexible EV Charging LP

A complete daily LP optimization model was developed.

Components:
- Building electrical load
- PV generation
- Battery Energy Storage System
- Flexible EV charging
- Electricity prices

Decision variables:
- EV charging powers
- Battery charging powers
- Battery discharging powers
- Battery state of charge
- Grid import powers
- Grid export powers

Constraints:
- Hourly power balance
- EV charging energy requirements
- EV connection windows
- Battery SOC dynamics
- Battery power limits
- SOC minimum and maximum limits
- Non-negative grid import/export

Objective:
- Minimize electricity purchase cost from the grid.

This notebook represents the first fully integrated optimization model combining all system components.
Results:

The integrated LP successfully coordinated PV generation,
battery storage and EV charging.

Observed behaviour:

- Battery discharged during periods with low available PV.
- Battery charged during hours with PV surplus.
- Battery discharged again during expensive evening hours.
- EV charging was shifted toward hours with high PV production.
- Grid imports were reduced during expensive periods.
- SOC limits were always respected.

The obtained schedule is physically realistic and economically meaningful.

The optimization successfully combines:

• Building demand
• PV generation
• Battery storage
• Flexible EV charging
• Electricity market prices

into a single optimal scheduling framework.
