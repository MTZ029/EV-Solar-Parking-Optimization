Notebook 12 – Multi-scenario comparison

The integrated LP was repeated for three representative EV charging days:
low-demand, medium-demand, and high-demand.

Results show that the medium-demand day has the highest cost, even though it does not have the highest EV demand.
This happens because PV production is very low on that day, forcing the system to rely heavily on grid import.

The high-demand day has the largest EV energy demand, but the cost remains low because PV production is also high
and the optimizer can use PV and BESS effectively.

This confirms that the operational benefit of smart charging depends not only on EV demand, but also on the
correlation between EV demand, PV availability, building load and electricity prices.

Scenario plot interpretation

The medium-demand scenario produces the highest operating cost and grid import.

This shows that EV demand alone does not determine system performance. The medium day has moderate EV charging
demand, but very low PV production, which forces the optimizer to import most of the required energy from the grid.

The high-demand scenario has the largest EV demand, but it also has high PV production. Therefore, the optimizer
can supply much of the demand using local PV generation and the battery.

The low-demand scenario has low EV demand and significant PV production, leading to low cost but also more PV
export.

Battery throughput remains similar across scenarios, indicating that the BESS is actively used in all cases.
